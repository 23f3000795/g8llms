## üè• Business Implications

### **1. Operational Risk**
Large swings in satisfaction scores imply inconsistent staffing, service quality, or patient wait times.

### **2. Financial Consequences**
Lower satisfaction correlates with:
- Reduced patient retention  
- Lower HCAHPS incentive payments  
- Potential reputational impact  

### **3. Strategic Importance**
To maintain competitive position and meet organizational KPIs, stabilizing patient experience is essential.

---

## üõ† Recommendations to Reach and Maintain 4.5+

### **1. Improve Service Quality**
- Implement standardized bedside communication protocols  
- Train staff in empathy, clarity, and expectation-setting  
- Increase rounding frequency  
- Assign patient-experience champions per shift  

### **2. Reduce Patient Wait Times**
- Introduce real-time queue dashboards  
- Optimize staffing schedules using patient-volume forecasts  
- Streamline triage and discharge workflows  

### **3. Reduce Score Variability**
- Introduce cross-location performance benchmarking  
- Weekly monitoring dashboards  
- Root-cause analysis for low-scoring weeks (especially Q1 periods)  

### **4. Governance & Accountability**
- Monthly leadership review  
- KPI ownership assigned to department heads  
- Incorporate satisfaction metrics into evaluation cycles
